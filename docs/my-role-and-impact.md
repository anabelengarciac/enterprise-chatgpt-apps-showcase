# My Role and Impact

## Role Summary

I joined the AI First team after completing my internship and contributed to the development of internal ChatGPT-based applications and MCP-style connectors connected to enterprise platforms and data sources through APIs.

My strongest contribution was acting as a bridge between business users and the technical team. I helped define, test, and iterate solutions so the apps were aligned with real business needs, not just technically functional.

## How I Added Value

### 1. Discovery and User Understanding

I worked directly with interested users to understand what they were trying to achieve, where existing workflows were slow or confusing, and what a useful ChatGPT app needed to deliver.

This included identifying:

- Frequent questions users wanted the app to answer.
- Business terminology the app needed to understand.
- Data and context users expected to see in the response.
- Adoption blockers such as unclear outputs, missing filters, or lack of trust in results.

### 2. Translating Business Needs into Technical Requirements

I helped convert user needs into practical requirements for the technical team. This meant clarifying not only what the app should do, but also how it should behave when information was missing, ambiguous, or unavailable.

Examples of this work included:

- Defining expected response formats and business-friendly explanations.
- Clarifying default assumptions when users did not specify all filters.
- Identifying edge cases from user conversations.
- Helping prioritize which capabilities mattered most for adoption.

### 3. Testing and Iteration

I participated in testing conversations and feedback sessions with users. My goal was to evaluate whether the app was actually useful from the user's perspective.

I looked for:

- Whether the answer matched the user's intent.
- Whether the app asked for clarification only when necessary.
- Whether technical outputs were understandable for business users.
- Whether data caveats and limitations were explained clearly.
- Whether users could continue the conversation naturally after the first answer.

### 4. Feedback Loop Design

I identified the need for a reusable feedback mechanism inside the ChatGPT apps. The goal was to make feedback easier to collect, easier to structure, and easier for the technical team to act on.

This became the concept of an in-chat feedback widget that could collect user comments and structured context at the moment an issue happened.

## Impact Narrative

My work helped reduce the gap between technically possible AI integrations and usable business products. The apps became stronger because user expectations, real workflows, and feedback loops were considered throughout the process.

For a technical team, I provided clearer business context and faster validation. For business users, I helped make the apps easier to understand, easier to test, and more aligned with the way they actually work.

## CV-Ready Summary

- Contributed to internal ChatGPT-based applications and MCP-style connectors integrated with enterprise platforms through APIs.
- Acted as a bridge between business stakeholders and technical teams across discovery, definition, testing, and iteration.
- Helped shape app behavior, user-facing outputs, feedback processes, and adoption-oriented workflows.
- Proposed a reusable in-chat feedback widget pattern to improve issue reporting and continuous product iteration.
